Self-test 11:
Ethics of AI

  1. (Simple) Which of the following are potential harms that AI systems may cause?

    1. Obtaining people's personal information without their consent.
    2. Producing wrong predictions due to model implementation issues.
    3. Obscuring the legal responsibilities

    1. (i) and (ii) only
    2. (i) and (iii) only
    3. (ii) and (iii) only
    4. (i), (ii) and (iii)

    Solution
    The answer is D.

  2. (Simple) Which of the following is NOT one of the 5 fundamental principles of AI as mentioned in the lectures??

    1. Autonomy
    2. Justice
    3. Non-maleficence
    4. Benevolence
    Solution
    The answer is D.

  3. (Medium) One of the problems with setting principles for AI is that the principles may conflict with each other. With regards to the collection of healthcare data, which 2 principles have a conflict?

    Solution
    Autonomy and Beneficence . People should control what private data they choose to share (autonomy). But fewer data may result in poorer performing models, so society at large may not benefit (beneficence).

  4. (Medium) State two ways to reduce the chances of introducing an unfair bias in an AI system.

    Solution
    • Ensure the training data used for the model is free from bias.
    • Performing regular tests and audits.
    • Ensure that the training data includes some samples from minority groups.

  5. (Medium) State one advantage and one disadvantage of using AI systems for resume screening.

    Solution
    • Advantage
      • AI systems can screen resumes faster.
      • It is cheaper to use AI systems compared with manually screening resumes.
    • Disadvantage
      • There may be gender bias.
      • There is a lack of transparency
      • Minority groups may be at a disadvantage.
